For better or worse, society favours those who are successful.

You can fight it, rationalise it, ignore it, or even pretend it doesn't mean anything: Ultimately your survival will depend on how successful you can be.

The problem is that attaining success in any field is difficult, irrational, cruel and deeply unnatural. It is a process that brings out the worst in even the best of us. Most of us fail, spin out and don't recover.

So what do you do?

Can't live with it. Can't live without it. That's what this website is about.

Hello.

Let me tell you a little bit about myself.

My name is Julius Reade and I created this website to help people understand and overcome the pitfalls of success.

Maybe you're looking to overcome your mental health issues, become an astrophyscist or even discover your sense of passion in life.

This website can help you.

Understandably, some of us got the shit end of the stick. We developed emotional problems, hung out with the wrong crowd, maybe even developed a few too many addictions and got attached to those who amplified the worst in us.

That's life and that's okay.

The good news is that humans can change. Yes, it's a messy process, however with the right guidance any individual can become motivated and ambitious enough to achieve great things. It's just a matter of practice and technique.

While I could list all the achievements and positive lifestyle changes I've made, these things are merely consequential to the primary thing that makes me feel like a success.

Ultimately, I feel safe and secure and I believe having control over my emotions and mental health issues is what makes me a success, as it is a foundation that allows me to do absolutely anything I want with my life.

Of course, everyone has their own challenges towards feeling safe and secure and that's what this website hopes to address.

To give you a sense who I am, I wake up at 6am each weekday to study. I study for two hours in the morning before work, as well as two hours after work, in addition to weightlifting three times a week.

This website aims to help by providing intuitive advice on what works and what doesn't based on my own experiences with life, so you can get it right the first time.

Because the reality is, success is not a natural. Avoiding burnout after weeks of intense study more than four hours each day is not natural. Overcoming years of mental health issues does not come naturally.

Certainly, I'm not perfect. I still get depressed. I still relapse. I get judgemental and I break things.

I'm still human in this robotic pursuit towards perfection, however that doesn't mean we cannot be effective the other 95% of the time.

It just requires a different way of thinking, so let's do it.
