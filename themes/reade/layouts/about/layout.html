For better or worse, society favours those who are successful.

You can fight it, rationalise it or even pretend it isn't a thing: Ultimately your survival will depend on it.

The problem is that attaining success in any field is a deeply cruel, difficult, irrational and unnatural process.

A process which often leads to addiction, depression, delusion and irrational obsession - rather than the apparent "success" we originally planned for.

In other words, we're basically fucked.

Can't live with it. Can't live without it. That's what this website is about.

Hello.

Let me tell you a little bit about myself.

My name is Julius Reade and I created this website to help people better understand and overcome the pitfalls of success.

Maybe you're looking to overcome your mental health issues, become a high-paying professional or even discover your sense of passion in life.

This website can help you.

Understandably, some of us got the shit end of the stick.

We developed emotional problems, hung out with the wrong crowd, maybe even developed a few too many addictions and got attached to those who amplified the worst in us.

That's life and that's okay.

Good news is that humans can change.

Yes, it's a cluster-fuck of a process, however with the right guidance leanings and guidance anyone can become motivated and ambitious enough to achieve great things. It's just a matter of practice and technique.

Of course most won't. Again, that's life and it's completely out of my control.

While I could list all the achievements and positive lifestyle changes I've made thus far, these things are merely consequential to the thing I consider most important.

Ultimately, I am successful because I have control over my emotions and mental health issues. And it is a foundation that allows me to do absolutely anything I want with my life.

Of course, everyone has their own challenges. These are simply my own.

To give you a sense who I am, I wake up at 6am each weekday to study. I study for two hours in the morning before work, as well as two hours after work, in addition to weightlifting three times a week.

I use this time to study programming and I'm going to stop at absolutely nothing to ensure that I become a professional programmer.

This website aims to help by providing intuitive advice on what works and what doesn't based on my own experiences in life.

Though perhaps this writing merely aims to help me and my career. Only time will tell.

Because the reality is, success is not natural.

Avoiding burnout from working full-time and studying more than four hours each day does not just "happen". Certainly, overcoming years of mental health issues doesn't happen overnight either.

Now I'm not perfect. I still get depressed. I still relapse. I still get judgemental and I break things, including the people around me.

Just because I'm human in this robotic pursuit towards intellectual automation, doesn't mean that I cannot be effective the other 95% of the time.

It just requires a different way of thinking, so let's do it.

Or don't. That doesn't change who I am.
